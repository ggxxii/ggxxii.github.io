Abstract

Texturing 3D humans has been a long-standing research area in human reconstruction and generation. However, acquiring high-quality human texture is a challenging and time-consuming task, resulting in a scarcity of such data in existing datasets. Recent methods have made attempts to incorporate large generative models into 3D human texture generation tasks, showing promising advancements. Nevertheless, current approaches still face challenges including slow generation speeds, limited text consistency, and subpar texture quality. In this paper, we introduce ATLAS (ArTicuLated humAn textureS), which is the first and largest high-resolution (1024x1024) 3D human texture dataset. It comprises 1,100 high-fidelity human textures, each accompanied by text descriptions, animation sequences, and composite rendering videos. Furthermore, we introduce TexDreamer, the first zero-shot multimodal high-fidelity 3D human texture generation model. By incorporating an efficient texture adaptation finetuning strategy and a novel feature translator module, our trained model is capable of generating high-fidelity 3D human textures from user prompts or images in seconds. We will make our dataset available for research purposes.


Pipeline

Pipeline for building our ATLAS dataset. Our pipeline mainly includes three processes, efficient texture adaption finetuning, diverse texture generation, and composite rendering. We obtain 91 high-quality sample textures with multi-view texture projection. Sample textures and prompts are used to train T2UV in TexDreamer. Then we infer T2UV to generate high-fidelity human textures that closely resemble 1,110 prompts generated by ChatGPT. The final composite rendering frame is rendered with background image and textured animation sequence using HDR lighting, PBR material, and perspective camera.
